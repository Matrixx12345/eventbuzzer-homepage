Switzerland may be small, but its museum landscape is world-class. From contemporary art to natural history to technical innovations – here are the 10 best museums you absolutely must visit.

## 1. Kunsthaus Zürich
Switzerland's largest art museum houses masterpieces by Monet, Picasso and Giacometti. The new Chipperfield extension makes it an architectural highlight.

## 2. Fondation Beyeler, Basel
In Riehen near Basel, the Fondation Beyeler showcases one of the most important collections of modern and contemporary art. The Renzo Piano building alone is worth the visit.

## 3. Kunstmuseum Bern
One of Switzerland's oldest art museums with a collection of over 3,000 paintings and sculptures. Particularly renowned for the Gurlitt Collection and works by Ferdinand Hodler.

## 4. Zentrum Paul Klee, Bern
The world's largest collection of Paul Klee works in a stunning building by Renzo Piano. Over 4,000 paintings, watercolors and drawings.

## 5. Swiss National Museum, Zurich
The entire history of Switzerland under one roof. From prehistory to the present – interactive and engaging.

## 6. Kunstmuseum Basel
The world's oldest publicly accessible art collection. Holbein, Warhol, Picasso – a journey through art history.

## 7. Natural History Museum, Bern
Over 200 dioramas with animals from around the world. Particularly famous: Barry, the legendary rescue dog from the Great St. Bernard Pass.

## 8. Olympic Museum, Lausanne
The history of the Olympic Games from antiquity to today. Interactive experiences and the largest collection of Olympic memorabilia.

## 9. Technorama Winterthur
The hands-on science center. Over 500 experiment stations make natural science tangible – perfect for families.

## 10. Museum Tinguely, Basel
Kinetic art by Jean Tinguely in a Mario Botta building. Machines that move, sound and fascinate.

## Tip: Museum Pass

With the Swiss Museum Pass, you get free admission to over 500 museums. Already worth it after 2-3 visits!
