Switzerland may be small in area, but its museum landscape is world-class. From contemporary art in architectural masterpieces to interactive journeys through time – there is something for every taste. We have put together the 10 museums for you that are absolutely not to be missed on a trip through Switzerland.

## 1. Kunsthaus Zürich

It is the largest art museum in Switzerland and a magnet for lovers of Classical Modernism. Here you will find masterpieces by Monet, Picasso, and Chagall. The museum is particularly proud of housing the world's most significant collection of works by Alberto Giacometti. Since 2021, the museum has also shone with the light-filled extension by David Chipperfield, which is worth a visit for its architecture alone.

## 2. Fondation Beyeler, Basel

Located in Riehen near Basel is one of the most beautiful museums in the world. The Fondation Beyeler showcases the high-caliber collection of Hildy and Ernst Beyeler, ranging from Classical Modernism to contemporary art. The building by Renzo Piano is set in an idyllic park and allows art and nature to merge – a place of absolute peace and inspiration.

## 3. Kunstmuseum Bern

As one of the oldest art museums in Switzerland, the Kunstmuseum Bern houses a world-class collection. Over 3,000 paintings and sculptures are found here, including the famous Gurlitt collection. A special focus is on works by Ferdinand Hodler and other significant Swiss artists, which are perfectly showcased in the historical setting.

## 4. Zentrum Paul Klee, Bern

No other museum in the world is so intensively dedicated to the work of Paul Klee. In a spectacular, wave-shaped architecture by Renzo Piano, parts of the collection of over 4,000 works are presented. Since Klee was an extremely productive artist, the exhibitions change constantly, so there is something new to discover with every visit.

## 5. Swiss National Museum, Zurich

Right behind Zurich's main station, a journey through time awaits. The Swiss National Museum is housed in probably the most beautiful castle building in the city and tells the story of Switzerland from prehistory to the present. The exhibitions are interactively designed and also offer exciting insights into Swiss culture and identity for children.

## 6. Kunstmuseum Basel

The Kunstmuseum Basel houses the oldest public art collection in the world. The range is enormous: from the Holbeins of the Renaissance to Andy Warhol and Jasper Johns in contemporary art. The modern extension is connected to the main building by an underground passage and provides space for spectacular special exhibitions.

## 7. Natural History Museum, Bern

A highlight for families and animal lovers. Over 200 dioramas show animals from all over the world in their natural environment. The most famous exhibit is undoubtedly "Barry," the legendary rescue dog from the Great St. Bernard Pass, who saved over 40 lives in the 19th century and has his place of honor here.

## 8. Olympic Museum, Lausanne

Lausanne is the capital of the Olympic Movement, and the Olympic Museum captures the spirit of the Games perfectly. From the torches of past games to the equipment of top athletes, everything can be seen here. Interactive stations allow you to experience the sporting challenges yourself and feel the values of excellence and fair play.

## 9. Technorama Winterthur

Science to touch and try – the Technorama is the largest science center in Switzerland. At over 500 experimental stations, visitors can discover natural phenomena playfully. There are no "Please do not touch" signs, only "Please try it out." A perfect destination for curious minds of all ages.

## 10. Museum Tinguely, Basel

Jean Tinguely was a master of kinetic art, and this museum celebrates his machine sculptures. In the striking building by Mario Botta right on the Rhine, the works come to life, clattering, squeaking, and moving. It is a fascinating experience that makes art accessible and entertaining for everyone.

## Tip: Museum Pass

If you plan to visit more than two museums in a year, the Swiss Museum Pass is your best friend. With it, you have free entry to over 500 museums throughout Switzerland. Often, the pass pays for itself after the third museum visit, and it motivates you to discover smaller, hidden gems as well.
